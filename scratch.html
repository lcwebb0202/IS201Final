Luke's Personal Project

Legal Practical

Luke and his fiancee recently purchased a 1966 Aristocrat Lo-Liner and are currently in the process of tranforming it into a mobile dirty soda/drink business. It has a lot of work to go but they are very excited about it.



Work that needs to be done includes:

  • Legal

    1. Create an LLC
    2. Get food handlers permit
    3. Get commissary agreement
    4. Get business permit
    5. Get mobile food permit

  • Practical

    1. Come up with business name
    2. Decide menu
    3. Create business plan
    4. Create marketing plan
    5. Create website
    6. Sand the wood on the trailer
    7. Remove the rust from the metal frame
    8. Paint the trailer
    9. Finish exterior decorations


They've have never had their own business before so it is a lot for them to learn together. From their research they believe that this type of business is perfect in the current market in Utah County.


Business Idea:

The main goal of the business is to fix the trailer up and make it look nice enough to cater weddings specificially. There are many different trailers/food trucks in the area that have proven the model and shown that people are willing to pay a premium for a service like this.


Luke and Shelby are excited to be able to pursue this business and are looking forward to the challenges and opportunities that come with it. They plan to be operational by the end of July.
